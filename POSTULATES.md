# TW3 Postulates

These are the formal axioms of TW3 — the community-ratified principles that bridge the [Philosophy](PHILOSOPHY.md) and the organizational documents that implement it.

Postulates exist to give the philosophy teeth. Where the philosophy states beliefs in prose, the postulates state rules in plain language. Every structural decision, governance mechanic, and smart contract must be consistent with these axioms. If it is not, the implementation must change.

## Authority

Postulates sit between the philosophy and the structure in TW3's hierarchy of authority:

1. **[PHILOSOPHY.md](PHILOSOPHY.md)** — source of truth
2. **This document (POSTULATES.md)** — formal axioms derived from the philosophy
3. **[STRUCTURE.md](STRUCTURE.md)** — roles and relationships
4. **[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md)** — decision-making mechanics
5. **[TOKENOMICS.md](TOKENOMICS.md)** — economic model and token mechanics
6. **Smart contracts and code** — implementation

Postulates may only be added, removed, or amended through a governance proposal that demonstrates the change better aligns with the philosophy. A postulate that contradicts the philosophy is invalid regardless of whether it was ratified by vote.

---

## I. Stakeholder sovereignty

Infrastructure belongs to those who build it, fund it, and use it — not to those who control its access. No organizational structure, contract, or governance mechanism may grant any individual or entity unilateral authority over a TW3-aligned project.

## II. Equal collective voice

Every stakeholder group holds equal total weight in governance decisions. No group may be created, merged, or restructured for the purpose of diluting or amplifying the voice of another group.

## III. Control cannot be purchased

Capital entitles the holder to economic yield and proportional voice within the investor group. It does not entitle the holder to governance majority. No amount of investment may override the collective will of those who build and use the project.

## IV. Open source without exception

Every project that receives TW3 support must be fully open source — inspectable, forkable, and operable without any proprietary dependency. If a project cannot be forked and run independently by anyone, it does not qualify.

## V. Forkability is a right

Any participant may fork any TW3-aligned project at any time. Forks are not acts of hostility — they are the mechanism by which communities correct course. A fork that better serves TW3 principles deserves TW3 support.

## VI. Power dilutes with growth

Structures must ensure that as a project attracts more participants, the influence of any single entity diminishes. Early participation earns voice proportional to contribution, not permanent outsized control.

## VII. Transparency is mandatory

Code, financials, governance decisions, and organizational structure must be publicly auditable. Stakeholders cannot make informed decisions — and markets cannot discipline bad actors — without full visibility.

## VIII. DAO governance is required

Projects that receive TW3 investment must be governed as decentralized autonomous organizations. Policy changes require community approval. No corporate hierarchies. No unilateral authority. Leaders govern with consent, not by right.

## IX. Labor before capital

When the interests of those who build and use a project conflict with the interests of those who fund it, the builders and users take priority. Contributors and users create the value that capital invests in — not the other way around.

## X. The philosophy supersedes all

Every postulate, structural decision, governance rule, and line of code serves the [Philosophy](PHILOSOPHY.md). If any implementation — including these postulates — produces outcomes that contradict the philosophy, the implementation must change. The philosophy is the source of truth. It is not overridden by vote, by code, or by convenience.
